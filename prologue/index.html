About

In addition to my work as a software developer, I'm a husband and father of two children, and so I come prepared with plenty of dad jokes. I also have a wide a variety of interests. I love playing basketball and have spent countless hours on the court. I've channeled this passion most recently the past several years and have volunteered coaching youth basketball and football, focusing on teamwork and developing fundamental skills. I'm also into art and music, whether it's painting portraits, going to rock concerts, or learning new guitar riffs or drum beats.

With my most recent venture back into the classroom, I've truly embraced the beginner's mind and am excited to continue learning and discovering.

Experience

Upon graduating from NC State University with a Bachelors in Art and Design, I began my professional career as a graphic artist in Southern California. A consulting opportunity fell into my lap and I soon found myself in Dublin, Ireland, where I was first introduced to web development. After only a few basic HTML classes and some self-study in CSS and Flash animation, I was tasked with designing and building websites for local Irish businesses. This is where I first learned that knowledge is only a few keystrokes away. Fortunately, my employer was pleased with my work and extended my three-month to just shy of two years.

Returning to the States, I worked as a graphic designer and web developer until a downturn in the field caused me to explore other career avenues. Curiously enough, I entered the legal profession as a court stenographer in New York City where I routinely transcribed testimony at speeds up to and beyond 225 words per minute, often in highly-contentious and confidential settings. This is where I honed my communication skills and became quite comfortable handling a room full of attorneys.

While I had great success as a court reporter, I always felt there was something else I needed to be doing, but I wasn't exactly sure what it was. With a move to Nashville and after some soul searching, I took a chance and enrolled in the full-stack web development bootcamp at Nashville Software School. It didn't take long for me to realize I had found what I was missing. I fell in love again with desiging and building web applications, troubleshooting and debugging code, and celebrating every single "Aha!" moment in between those gloriously frustrating periods of "Why doesn't my code work?"
